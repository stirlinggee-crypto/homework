Silurian Period

Giant ice sheets from the previous period melted, making the Earth warmer and seas rise, creating shallow, tropical environments.


Landmasses collided, forming early mountains, and volcanic islands dotted the seas.


The Silurian was a big step for life, as plants and animals started colonizing land, setting the stage for all future life on Earth.


Millipedes and scorpions were among the first animals to crawl onto land, using their hard exoskeletons for protection and to keep moisture in.
