Triassic Period

All the land was joined into one giant continent called Pangea, meaning "all Earth".


Furry, mammal-like reptiles called synapsids were around, and they are our distant ancestors.


The very first dinosaurs were small, walked on two legs, and looked a bit like fast lizards, with early examples like Eoraptor and Herrerasaurus.


Other reptiles like early crocodiles, pterosaurs (flying reptiles), ichthyosaurs (dolphins), and plesiosaurs (long-necked swimmers) also started in the Triassic.
