Carboniferous Period

High oxygen levels meant insects grew huge, like giant dragonflies with wingspans as big as your hand and huge millipedes.


Instead of grass, there were enormous ferns, horsetails, and clubmosses that looked like giant trees.


Reptiles first appeared during this time, evolving from amphibians and learning to lay eggs on land.


Plants died, but didn't rot away quickly because the bacteria and fungi that break down wood hadn't evolved yet, allowing them to pile up and form coal.
