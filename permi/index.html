Permian Period

This period marks the end of the Paleozoic Era. It was a time when all major landmasses assembled into the supercontinent Pangaea, leading to a drier climate.


The air had so much oxygen (around 35% compared to today's 21%) that bugs grew huge! You could find millipedes as big as crocodiles and dragonflies with wingspans over two feet.


Amphibians ruled the land, and the first reptiles appeared, with skin and eggs that let them live away from water.


There was no grass or flowering plants back then, just giant trees and ferns.
