Precambrian era

Precambrian era is the big, early chapter of Earth's history, covering nearly 90% of its life, from its creation around 4.6 billion years ago until the Cambrian Period (541 million years ago)


The Precambrian era is roughly 4 billion years long.


Most living animals were simple, single-celled organisms, and complex life forms didn't appear until later periods.


Great Oxidation Event was Around 2.4 billion years ago, bacteria began producing oxygen through photosynthesis, greatly changing the atmosphere with carbon dioxide to make oxygen.
