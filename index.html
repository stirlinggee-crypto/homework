Fossils

Fossils are the preserved remains of ancient plants and animals found in rock layers and give information about Earth's past life and evolution.

Fossils are made when organisms are quickly buried in mud or sand, stopping them decaying, and their hard parts like bones and shells are turned into rock.

As well as plants and animals, fossils can also include footprints, burrows, and other clues about the past.
