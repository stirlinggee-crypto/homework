Devonian Period

Some fish developed bony fins that were strong enough to help them move on land, becoming the first amphibians, like giant salamanders.


Plants grew bigger and spread, creating the first forests with trees and soil, changing the planet.


The supercontinent Gondwana moved north, and Euramerica (North America/Europe) formed, smashing together to create mountains.


The Devonian ended with several extinction events, where many ocean creatures, especially corals, disappeared, possibly due to low oxygen in the seas.
