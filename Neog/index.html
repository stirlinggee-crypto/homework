Neogene Period

As the Earth continued to cool and dry, vast forests were replaced by open grasslands and savannas.


Mammals and birds evolved into forms that look very similar to those we see today. This was the era of the giant Megalodon shark in the oceans and early ancestors of horses, dogs, and cats on land.


Toward the end of the Neogene, the first early human ancestors (hominins) appeared in Africa.


The most significant geographic event was the formation of the Isthmus of Panama, which connected North and South America for the first time. This allowed animals to migrate between the two continents in an event called the "Great American Faunal Interchange".
